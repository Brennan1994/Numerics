Detailed Description

A class for Trapezoidal rule integration. Integration steps are refined until convergence.

Authors: Haden Smith, USACE Risk Management Center, cole..nosp@m.h.sm.nosp@m.ith@u.nosp@m.sace.nosp@m..army.nosp@m..mil

Description: In its most basic form, the Trapezoidal rule approximates the region under the graph of f(x) as a trapezoid and calculating its area as:

b
∫ f(x) dx ~ T(a, b) = (b - a) * [ f(a) + f(b) ] / 2
a

This method can also be seen as averaging the left and right Riemann sums. Moreover, the interval can be subdivided to improve the accuracy of the approximation, with the trapezoidal rule applied to every sub-interval. Here the integration steps are refined, i.e. the interval is subdivided, until convergence.
